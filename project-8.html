Verve Lifestyle Card

ClientVerve
Role3D Product Animation
Year2025
ToolsCinema 4D, After Effects

The Brief

Verve needed to introduce the Lifestyle Card in a way that felt as considered as the card itself. Flat product photography wasn't going to capture the finish, weight, or detail that makes it feel premium in hand, so I built a 3D animation that could sell that feeling in a few seconds of screen time, for use across launch and social.

I pushed past a straightforward turntable render. The goal was something that felt directed — a small piece of film with its own pacing, not a spec sheet in motion.

Concept & Direction

I made the card itself the main character. Every decision — camera path, lighting rig, timing — was built around giving it room to actually be looked at: no competing graphics, no rushed cuts. The brief called for confidence, so I leaned the direction into stillness and slow reveal instead of fast, flashy motion.

01

Lighting & Camera

Most of the real work happened here. I tuned a soft key light and controlled reflections to catch the card's edge detail and surface finish without blowing out highlights, then paired it with slow, deliberate camera moves — the kind that let the material do the talking.

02

Material & Finish

Getting the surface to read correctly took several passes. I balanced how matte and reflective areas caught light until the render felt like a real object, not a CG stand-in, matching Verve's actual card finish as closely as I could get it.

03

Animation & Rhythm

I built the final cut around a clean, confident rhythm — entrances and holds timed so the card always gets a moment to breathe before the next move. Premium, not a quick product spin.

Outcome

The result is a piece that carries the Lifestyle Card launch on its own — clean enough for a keynote moment, short enough to hold attention on social, and built to make the card feel exactly as premium on screen as it does in hand.
