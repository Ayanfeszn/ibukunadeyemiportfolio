Verve Lifestyle Card

ClientVerve
Role3D Product Animation
Year2025
ToolsCinema 4D, After Effects

The Brief

Verve needed a way to introduce the Lifestyle Card that felt as considered as the card itself. Flat product photography couldn't capture the finish, weight, or detailing that make the physical card feel premium in hand — so the ask was a 3D animation that could sell that feeling in a few seconds of screen time, for use across launch and social.

Rather than a straightforward turntable render, the goal was something that felt directed — a small piece of film with its own pacing, not just a spec sheet in motion.

Concept & Direction

The card itself became the main character. Every decision — camera path, lighting rig, timing — was built around giving it room to be looked at properly: no competing graphics or text pulling focus away from the object, no rushed cuts. The brief called for confidence, so the direction leaned into stillness and slow reveal rather than fast, flashy motion.

01

Lighting & Camera

Most of the work happened here. A soft key light and controlled reflections were tuned to catch the card's edge detailing and surface finish without blowing out highlights, then paired with slow, deliberate camera moves — the kind that let the material do the talking instead of the motion.

02

Material & Finish

Getting the card's surface to read correctly in 3D took several passes — balancing how matte and reflective areas caught light so the render felt like a real object rather than a CG stand-in, matching Verve's actual card finish as closely as possible.

03

Animation & Rhythm

The final cut was built around a clean, confident rhythm — entrances and holds timed so the card always had a moment to breathe before the next move, keeping the piece feeling premium rather than like a quick product spin.

Outcome

The final animation gave Verve a piece of content that could carry the Lifestyle Card launch on its own — clean enough for a keynote moment, short enough to hold attention on social, and built to make the card feel exactly as premium on screen as it does in hand.
